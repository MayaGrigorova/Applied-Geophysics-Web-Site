Geophysical methods

  • Electrical methods

    This geophysical technique is used for imaging sub-surface structures from electrical resistivity measurements made at the ground surface.

    It is very useful technique for sub-surface imaging.

  • Gravity methods

    Gravity is a geophysical technique used to detect and identify subsurface bodies and anomalies within the Earth, based on the difference of their densities.

    It is based on the density changes of rock bodies and their effect on the acceleration of gravity.

  • Seismic methods

    The basic principle of all seismic methods is the controlled generation of elastic waves by a seismic source in order to obtain an image of the subsurface.

    Seismic methods depend upon velocities of acoustical energy in earth materials.

  • Magnetic methods

    Magnetic methods are based upon measuring the magnetic effects produced by varying concentrations of ferromagnetic minerals, in geological formations.

    Magnetic geophysical surveys measure small, localised variations in the Earth's magnetic field.

  • Well logging methods

    Well logging, also known as borehole logging is the practice of making a detailed record (a well log) of the geologic formations penetrated by a borehole.

    Well logging is performed in boreholes drilled for the oil and gas, groundwater, mineral and geothermal exploration, as well as part of environmental and geotechnical studies.

  • Radiometric methods

    The radiometric, or gamma-ray spectrometric method is a geophysical process used to estimate concentrations of the radioelements by measuring the gamma-rays.

    All rocks and soils contain radioactive isotopes, and almost all the gamma-rays detected near the Earth's surface are the result of the natural radioactive decay of potassium, uranium and thorium.
